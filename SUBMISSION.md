# MMC: a mechanistic-hypothesis engine a target team can trust

Most drugs fail because the target was wrong, and target teams cannot trust black-box AI on
the newest disease atlases. MMC is an AI biological engineer that reads the Zhu 2025
genome-scale CD4+ T-cell Perturb-seq atlas, autonomously proposes interpretable, runnable
models of disease-driving regulatory circuits (signed edges and simulatable logic), tests
them against the real perturbation data, and refuses to certify mechanism that does not beat a
simple baseline. On the Th2/GATA3 allergy axis it recovers a coherent circuit; on cytokine
production it proposes STK11/LKB1 as a chemokine regulator. A positive control keeps the claim
honest: the STK11 edges are individually grounded, flagged predictively necessary like textbook
edges, yet the model built from them does not beat a linear baseline held-out (0.18 versus
0.45), and the held-out gate refuses to certify it on that basis. Plausible, grounded mechanism
is not predictive advantage over a baseline, and the gate reveals the gap. The engine ships a
limit-map of where mechanism can and cannot be trusted, across single-perturbation and
combinatorial regimes. No prediction win or disease discovery is claimed: the contribution is
trustworthy, testable mechanistic hypotheses and an AI that will not send teams chasing
plausible-but-unvalidated targets. Scope: CD4+ T cells, these modules, this atlas.
