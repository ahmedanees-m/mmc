# MMC: a mechanistic-hypothesis engine a target team can trust

Most drugs fail because the target was wrong, and target teams cannot trust black-box AI on
the newest disease atlases. MMC is an AI biological engineer that reads the Zhu 2025
genome-scale CD4+ T-cell Perturb-seq atlas, autonomously proposes interpretable, runnable
models of disease-driving regulatory circuits (signed edges and simulatable logic), tests
them against the real perturbation data, and refuses to certify the hypotheses it cannot
stand behind. On the Th2/GATA3 allergy axis it recovers a coherent circuit; on cytokine
production it proposes STK11/LKB1 as a chemokine repressor with a data-grounded rationale,
then its own held-out gate rejects it because it does not predict. The self-correction is
measured: across every hypothesis the loop proposed, nine coherently-argued proposals yielded
zero that survived held-out validation, a 100 percent gate catch rate. Plausibility did not
track prediction; the gate supplied the calibration. The engine ships a limit-map declaring
where mechanism can and cannot be trusted. No prediction win or disease discovery is claimed:
the contribution is trustworthy, testable mechanistic hypotheses and an AI that will not send
teams chasing plausible-but-wrong targets. Scope: CD4+ T cells, these modules, this atlas.
